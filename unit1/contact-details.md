# Key Contacts

Work progresses more smoothly when you know who are the stakeholders to the work and how to contact them. Identify as many points of contact as possible to avoid losing momentum when people are not available.

## Contact Groups

### Primary Point of Contact

| Organization | ----- |

| When to contact | At what points in the work or for what types of issues should the person be contacted? |
| Reviewer | Is the representative a client reviewer who has final approval over a particular aspect of the work?[^2] |

## Notes

[^1]: See [National Numbering Plans](https://www.itu.int/oth/T0202.aspx?parent=T0202) of the International Telecommunication Union for a list of international dialing codes.
[^2]: Identifying if client reviews will take place in localization production is an important aspect of scope management. Establish who the client reviewers are within an organization, if any, to avoid surprise reviews that contribute to rework and increase costs and timelines on projects. It is recommended to get client approval over: 1. terminology, 2. translations, and 3. localization.
